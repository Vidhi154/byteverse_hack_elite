FAMOUS-FOOD

Vada-Pav

Vada pav, alternatively spelt wada pao, is a vegetarian fast food dish native to the Indian state of Maharashtra. The dish consists of a deep fried potato dumpling placed inside a bread bun sliced almost in half through the middle. It is generally accompanied with one or more chutneys and a green chili pepper.

Mawa-Samosa

Mewa Samosa is a dry fruit mini samosa. These crispy fried triangles stuffed with the amalgamation of sweet n salty spices and richness of finest quality dry fruits. These mini items are an instant hit for any occasion. Especially popular during the festive season of Diwali, Holi, Karwa Chauth etc. Commonly consumed as a tea time snack.

Baida- Roti

Baida roti is an egg-based paratha package, and Baida signifies egg. For this food, people use Refined flour to form the paratha dough, which is then filled with a mixture of chopped chicken, egg, and other spices. They then fold it into a bundle, fry it quickly on a Tava (griddle), and serve it with a squeeze of lime

Akuri-Toast

Scrambled eggs made in the Parsi style recipe are also referred to as Akuri Recipe. Parsi Community of India makes this delectable dish where eggs are not overcooked. Instead, the eggs are little runny and loose. Mild spices are added to it to make it flavoursome.
